History of the font

Movement is a variable font inspired by dance movements, created by Noel Pretorius and María Ramos of NM Type, in collaboration with Design Indaba and South African dancer Andile Vellem. The typeface draws inspiration from the art of dance, specifically the movements of a dancer, and the theories of choreographer Rudolf Laban. NM Type collaborated with Design Indaba and South African dancer Andile Vellem to create the typeface. The designers were inspired by Laban's concept of movement as the "inner intention" of the dancer. They tracked Andile's movements and used those paths as a reference for the letter shapes during the design process. The typeface represents direct movement with straight strokes and indirect movement with curved shapes. It's a variable font with four design extremes: Direct Black, Direct Thin, Indirect Black, and Indirect Thin, with variants in between representing changes in movement over time.
